About Me.



Education and Motivation

I'm an aspiring data scientist who graduated from UCLA with a degree in biochemistry. Although originally a premed student, while conducting my senior year internship at a biotech company, InjectSense, I was exposed to programming and the growing impact of machine learning in healthcare.

As I learned more from the company, my fascination grew. I read studies about how people were creating algorithms that could analyze images from MRIs or X-rays to find deformities imperceptible to doctors, or how they could predict the way drugs would interact with certain DNA sequences streamlining expensive, time-consuming drug trials and lab work.

After I graduated I took a flexible EMT position where I could teach myself coding while keeping the medical or graduate school route open. It wasn't long before it became clear to me that programming, especially manipulating and analyzing datasets, was something that I found especially stimulating and enjoyable. Currently, I am committed to looking for a job in data analytics with the goal of working before applying to graduate school for data science.
